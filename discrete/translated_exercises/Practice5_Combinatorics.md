# Practice 5: Combinatorics - Basic Rules

## 1. Fundamental Rules of Combinatorics (Sum and Product Rules)

1. Find the count of integers from 0 to 1000 that contain at least one digit 6.

2. Find the count of integers from 0 to 1000 that contain one or more digits 6. (Seems duplicate of 1)

3. In how many ways can you choose two books of different subjects if there are 15 books on Informatics, 12 on Mathematics, and 10 on Physics?

4. How many odd numbers are there between 100 and 1000?

5. How many natural numbers less than 700 are divisible by 5? How many of those are divisible by 3? How many are divisible by both 3 and 5?

6. If there are 15 flights from Yerevan to Moscow and 20 flights from Moscow to London, how many ways can you fly from Yerevan to London via Moscow?

7. Find the number of 4-digit numbers that have at least two identical digits.

8. Find the number of 7-digit numbers where the first and last digits are different.

9. Find the number of 6-digit numbers where adjacent digits are different.

10. Find the number of 7-digit numbers where even positions have odd digits and odd positions have even digits.

## 2. Permutations and Arrangements (Kargavoruyunner)

1. How many mappings are there from a 6-element set to a 3-element set? ($3^6$)

2. How many injective mappings are there from a 6-element set to a 3-element set? (0, by Pigeonhole Principle)

3. In how many ways can 5 boys and 6 girls stand in a line such that boys occupy the first 5 positions?

4. In how many ways can 6 boys and 7 girls stand in a line such that the first position is occupied by a boy?

5. In how many ways can 6 boys and 7 girls stand in a line such that the first and last positions are occupied by boys?

6. In how many ways can 5 boys and 6 girls stand in a line such that boys occupy 5 consecutive positions?

7. In how many ways can 9 people be arranged in a line? ($9!$)

8. How many 8-digit numbers can be formed using the digits of the number 23122313? (Check digits: 1, 2, 2, 3, 3...)

9. Find the number of words of length 6 formed from the alphabet $\{ a, b, g, d, e, z \}$ where two vowels are adjacent. (Determine vowels: a, e. Consonants: b, g, d, z)

10. Prove that in any group of 6 people, there are either 3 mutual friends or 3 mutual strangers (Ramsey number $R(3,3)=6$).

11. How many numbers must be selected from the first 210 natural numbers to guarantee that at least one even number is selected?

12. Prove that among the first 2014 terms of the sequence 7, 77, 777, ... there is one that is divisible by 2013. (Pigeonhole principle)
