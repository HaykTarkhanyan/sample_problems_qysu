# Practice 7: Partitions & Inclusion-Exclusion

1. In how many ways can a 5-element set be partitioned into 3 non-empty classes? (Bell numbers / Stirling numbers of the second kind)

2. In how many ways can $m$ distinct balls be distributed into $n$ distinct boxes such that no box is empty? (Surjective mappings, inclusion-exclusion)

3. How many numbers between 1 and 1000 are not divisible by 2, 3, or 5?

4. How many 4-digit numbers contain at least one digit 7?

5. How many integers between 1 and 16500 are not divisible by 5?

6. How many integers between 1 and 16500 are not divisible by 5 and not divisible by 3 (neither)?

7. How many integers between 1 and 16500 are not divisible by 5 and not divisible by 3, but are divisible by 11?

8. How many integers between 1 and 16500 are not divisible by 5, 3, and 11?

9. In a class of 30 students, 20 know English, 12 know French, and 6 know both languages. How many students know neither language?

10. Each side of triangle ABC is divided into 8 equal segments. How many triangles can be formed with vertices at these division points such that their sides are parallel to the sides of ABC?

11. Three carpets of area 3 m² each are placed in a room of area 6 m². Prove that there exist two carpets that overlap by at least 1/2 m²? (Pigeonhole / Area principle).

12. Find the number of integer solutions to the system:
    $x_1 + x_2 + x_3 = 40$
    $4 \le x_1 \le 15$
    $9 \le x_2 \le 18$
    $5 \le x_3 \le 16$
