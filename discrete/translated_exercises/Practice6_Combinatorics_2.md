# Practice 6: Combinations and Binomial Coefficients

## 2.4. Combinations

1. How many bit strings of length 9 contain 5 zeros and 4 ones?

2. In how many ways can a committee of 6 teachers and 8 students be formed from 12 teachers and 20 students?

3. In how many ways can you choose 13 cards from a deck of 52 such that they include 5 cards of the same suit?

4. In how many ways can you divide 10 people into two teams of 5 people each?

5. Let $A = \{ a, b, c, d, e, f, g, h \}$. How many 5-element subsets of $A$ contain the element 'a'?

6. Find the coefficient of $x^6 y^4$ in the expansion of $(2x + 3y)^{10}$.

7. On a chessboard, a rook is at square a1. In how many ways can it reach square e5 if it can only move right or up?

8. Find the number of paths from $O(0, 0)$ to $A(7, 8)$ in a grid that pass through the point $(3, 4)$.

## 2.5. Combinations with Repetition

9. Find the number of ways to partition the number 6 into three summands.

10. A store sells three types of cakes. In how many ways can you buy 7 cakes?

## 3. Ordered Partitions and Multinomial Coefficients

11. How many different words can be formed by rearranging the letters of "matematika"?

12. In how many ways can you arrange 15 math books, 16 informatics books, and 12 physics books on a shelf if books of the same subject are indistinguishable?

13. What is the coefficient of $x_1^4 x_2^3 x_3^2$ in the expansion of $(x_1 + x_2 + x_3)^9$?
